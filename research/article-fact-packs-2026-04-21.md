## Article 1: Chloramine in Orlando/Kissimmee Tap Water

- **OUC**: Ozone primary disinfectant + chlorine residual. Ozone also removes H2S. (ouc.com/about/water-services)
- **Toho Water Authority**: Chloramines confirmed. Notice March 2026 about temporary switch to free chlorine for Harmony area maintenance (5–7 day process). Serves 100,000+ customers in Kissimmee, Poinciana, unincorp Osceola.
- **Orange County Utilities**: Likely free chlorine. Verify against 2024 OCU Annual Drinking Water Report before claiming chloramines.
- **Typical chloramine residuals in FL systems:** 1.5–3.5 ppm. EPA MRDL = 4.0 ppm.
- **Why catalytic carbon required:**
  - Standard GAC: ~40–65% chloramine removal at residential flow (physical adsorption only)
  - Catalytic carbon: enhanced surface chemistry catalyzes chloramine breakdown → chloride + nitrogen
  - Chloramine N-Cl bond must be chemically decomposed, not just adsorbed

## Article 2: Sinkholes & Karst Geology — Central Florida Wells

- **Karst fundamentals:** Carbonic acid dissolves limestone/dolomite of Floridan Aquifer; voids reach tens of feet
- **Floridan Aquifer:** ~100,000 sq mi; drinking water for ~10M across FL, GA, AL, SC, MS
- **Sinkholes breach confining clay layer:** Rapid downward movement of surface water + contaminants
- **Polk County — Highland City, Royal Crest Drive:** ~70 ft deep / 15 ft wide sinkhole January 2024; SECOND sinkhole at same property November 2024 (~8 ft wide × 20 ft deep)
- **FDEP Delineated Areas Program:** Ch. 373 F.S. Part III; Ch. 62-524 F.A.C.
- New wells in delineated contamination zones need stringent construction + mandatory testing by County Health Dept before potable-use clearance
- Florida DOH Well Surveillance Program monitors private-well threats

## Article 5: PFAS in Central Florida Water

**EPA PFAS rule status (CRITICAL):**
- Final April 10, 2024; FR April 26, 2024
- PFOA MCL = 4.0 ppt; PFOS MCL = 4.0 ppt
- PFHxS, PFNA, HFPO-DA: 10 ppt individually (originally)
- **UPDATED May 14, 2025:** EPA retained PFOA/PFOS MCLs, EXTENDED compliance to **2031** (from 2029). Proposed to revoke PFHxS/PFNA/HFPO-DA/PFBS standards pending re-evaluation.
- Initial monitoring by 2027

**Orange County Utilities PFAS detections:**
- **Malcolm Road facility ~4.7 ppt**
- **County Road 535 facility ~5 ppt**
- Both ABOVE 4 ppt MCL
- County approved $4.5M contract with Tetra Tech for PFAS remediation engineering (Feb 2025)

**OUC:** PFAS sampling April 2025. Sources deep lower Floridan Aquifer (~quarter-mile depth) — typically lower PFAS exposure.

**Toho Water:** Participates in UCMR5 and FDEP initial monitoring. Do not assert Toho MCL exceedances without direct verification.

**Orlando MCO / AFFF:**
- No specific MCO contamination study found
- Frame as "potential legacy AFFF source area consistent with national airport pattern"
- FAA ordered transition to fluorine-free foam (F3) starting 2023

**Treatment effectiveness (EPA-confirmed BATs):**
- RO: up to 99% removal of PFAS6
- GAC: reduces below detection; better on long-chain; requires regular media changeouts
- Ion exchange (anion resin): 94–99% of PFAS6; better on short-chain than GAC; single-use disposal
